Pageantry | Pale North

Nic's Pitch | 2020/02/05

premise

The video is loosely about a trans woman looking back at her younger self, but without forming a concrete narrative.
It is intended to be universally recognizable to trans people without becoming rote or generic, and still putting forth a deliberate sense of style and aesthetic.




outline

INT BEDROOM - DAY
We whip-pan into the bedroom of THE KID (late-teens, dressed in boys clothes - we never clearly see their face). They lie in bed, sheets wet from tears. Tawny sunlight fills the space. There is a quiet stillness.
BRIE is suddenly standing there, ethereally watching over The Kid. She sits softly on the bed, and put a hand on their shoulder.

We also get some shots of Brie, sitting alone in the room, singing.

EXT ALLEYWAY - DUSK
We start cutting to the band walking confidently down the an alley, in slow motion, singing.

INT GRUNGY VENUE - NIGHT
We don't see much but darkness, party lights, and The Kid leaning timidly against the wall.
We see closeups of manic feet dancing on the concrete, sifting glitter every which way.

In slow motion, The Kid walks down the road, passing in and out of streetlight beams. Hands in pockets, they look crumpled.
The whole band is suddenly a few meters behind them, all confidently walking in slow motion.
They walk together a while. Suddenly, The Kid stops and turns around, facing the band, making eye contact with Brie, The Kid starts walking towards her, then running. The band continues confidently walking.
The Kid rips past them and keeps running. Brie smiles.
Cut back to normal speed, The Kid barrels down the street, and eventually off into the dark, as the song ends.



style

There will be an inherent grittiness due the budget of the production, but we can embrace that for a sense of authenticity.
The video is a mix of slow, otherworldly moments (meant to contrast the song) and kinetic, energized beats (meant to emphasize it). It includes a mix of standard 24p footage, slomo 60p, and step-printed 12p.

notes

I've written this with low resources in mind. It's generally seen as distastful to ask local crew to volunteer on music videos, so this would be more of a friends / family affair. Thankfully, I like small crews, and friends. I'd be taking care of directing, camera, and post.
